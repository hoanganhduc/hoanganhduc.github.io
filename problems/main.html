1 Preliminaries

For the terminology and notation not defined here, see the textbooks by Diestel [1] or West [2]. We often denote by Pn,Cn,Kn,Km,n the path, cycle, complete graph, complete bipartite graph on n vertices, respectively. For two graphs G,H, the disjoint union of G and H, denoted by G+H, is the graph with V(G+H)=V(G)∪V(H) and E(G+H)=E(G)∪E(H).

2 Reconfiguration of Independent Sets under Token Sliding

2.1 Definitions

Let G be a simple, undirected graph. The double-broom graph Dr,n,s is the tree obtained from a path Pn=v1v2…vn by attaching r leaves to v1 and s leaves to vn, where r,n,s are positive integers. Two independent sets I,J are adjacent under Token Sliding (𝖳𝖲) if there exists u,v∈V(G) such that I∖J={u}, J∖I={v}, and uv∈E(G). The Token Sliding problem asks whether there is a 𝖳𝖲-sequence between I and J in G, that is, a sequence of independent sets starting with I and ending with J where any two consecutive members are adjacent under 𝖳𝖲. For a fixed positive integer k, the graph 𝖳𝖲k(G) contains all size-k independent sets as its vertices/nodes, and two nodes are joined by an edge if their corresponding independent sets are adjacent under 𝖳𝖲.

2.2 Conjecture/Question

Open Problem 1.

What is the complexity of Token Sliding on outerplanar graphs? And more generally, on series-parallel graphs (≡ graphs of treewidth at most two)?

Conjecture 2.

Let G be a forest. Then 𝖳𝖲k(G) is a forest if and only if G is {2K2+(k-2)K1,D2,2,2+(k-2)K1,D2,4,2+(k-3)K1}-free, for some integer k≥4.
